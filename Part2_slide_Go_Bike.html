Effect of Various Features on Ford GoBike Trips Durations¶

by (Bankole Ridwan Abiola)¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the featrues of Ford GoBike sharing system that could be used to predict the trip durations. The main focus was on the user birth year, start and end time of trip, user gender and user type.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consisted of record for about 183,412 trips and 16 features including duration(seconds), user information and station information. 197 of the record had no information about station, 8265 had no users information and 16 out of them did not have both.

Distribution of Trip Durations¶

Trip durations in the dataset take on a very large range of values, from about \ 61 secs at the lowest, to about 84548 seconds at the highest. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, \ the distribution of trip durations took on a unimodal shape with most of the trips took \ 1000 seconds or less.

Distribution of Users' Age¶

Most of the users aged between 20 and 40.

Distribution of Users' Gender¶

About 70% of the users are male

Distribution of Trips Start Day¶

There were more trips during weekdays than weekend which could imply frequent use of bike to work.

Distribution of Trip Start Hours¶

Most of the trips occurred between 7:00am and 9:00am which was when users were likely to go to work and between 4:00pm and 7:00pm when they were likely to close from work. This could imply frequent use of bike to avoid traffic during the rush hours.

Trip durations vs. Users' Age¶

Plotting trip durations against users' age showed that trips duration greater than 60,000 seconds were covered by users between age 20 and 30; also, no user younger than 20 years or older than 60 years ever travelled a duration higher than 10,000 seconds.

Duration and age by user gender¶

There was a similar trend in trip duration against age for both male and female. However, male distribution was slightly to higher age than female. Therefore there were more male users older than 60 years than male. Also, high trip durations were more common among male than female.

Duration and start day by user type¶

The number of customer that used bike per day was far less than subscriber but customer rode for a far longer duration per day than subscriber. Also, average duration per day remained fairly constant throughout the week for subscriber, this could mean subscribers were limited to a fixed trip duration in accordance to their subscription; but customers rode for a longer duration in accordance with their need at the moment.

Conclusion¶

The trip durations was found to be influenced by the age of the users, their gender, the user type they belong, they day and the time of the day.

  • Users between age 20 and 40 covered most of the trip durations
  • Though Male travelled most of the high trip durations recorded, female travelled on average for a higher durations than male
  • Customers travelled for a longer durations than subscribers
  • Users travel higher duration during the rushing hours in the morning (7:00am - 9:00am) and in the evening (4:00pm and 7:00pm) when they were likely going to work and returning home respectively.
